Bit House Saloon

One of Bit House's main strengths is the sheer variety of drinks it offers. Interested in creative cocktails? The Southeast Portland bar has you covered with classics made right, thoughtful original drinks, and a list of Old Fashioneds. More of a whiskey gal? Bit House's selection is comparable to that of a dedicated whiskey bar. Looking to sample Portland's highly praised beer scene? There are plenty of brews on tap. Perhaps a little surprisingly, the heavily-wooded, pre-Prohibition era space is also rumored to be the origin of "frosé;" even if that's not quite the case, it was certainly one of the first to mix rosé and spirits and serve the slushy mixture.

Multnomah Whiskey Library

At Multnomah Whiskey Library, there are cocktails, to be sure, most of which are whiskey-based. There's even beer and wine. But to order those would be a bewildering choice. MWL offers the most robust selection of whiskeys in the state, from bourbons you might see at a neighborhood dive, to rare, vintage scotches and esoteric Japanese varieties. Some come with a hefty price tag; it's to each their own to decide whether it's worth the splurge. If you need some sound advice about what to order, you're in luck: The staff here is rigorously trained in both service and knowledge.

Bible Club Pdx

Going to Bible Club, in sleepy Sellwood/Moreland, feels like taking a journey back in time. Every single thing in the bar, from the floor to the ceiling, the furniture to the door knobs, the bar equipment to the lighting, dates back to the 1930s—at the latest. Most of it is actually from the 19th century—even the tunes predate Prohibition. It's often referred to, both by its owners and local media, as a "museum you can drink in." Another notable feature is the price of the cocktails; unusually for Portland, none cost less than $12, and many hit the $16 mark. Then again, each drink incorporates high-quality ingredients and high-end booze. If you're looking to really splash out, ask about the secret Seven Deadly Sins menu ($30 to $100), where drinks like the Robber Baron will ruin you for any other Old Fashioned.

Devils Point

Portland's strip club culture is uniquely progressive, and it's a deeply ingrained part of the city's bar culture as a whole. Devils Point is a perfect example of this, with its incredibly talented dancers and raucous vibes—there's nothing else like it. If you're expecting a cliché strip club crowd, you'll be surprised. Rather than sketchy-looking businessman throwing piles of dollar bills onstage, the crowd here is indistinguishable from any other neighborhood bar, with men, women, groups, and couples all rallying around to enjoy the drinks and entertainment in the progressive, upbeat atmosphere. Grab a domestic tallboy—never a fancy cocktail—and join them.
